Photography and Private Life:
Identity and Eros: A Photographer's Workshop

Taught by Holly Smith Pedlosky
Radcliffe Seminars
Radcliffe Institute for Advanced Study, Harvard University

Friday March 23, 2001
10 am-3:30 pm


This Seminar has two parts. In the morning, Holly Smith Pedlosky will present a slide show about photographs of personal life including work by nineteenth century photographers like Julia Margaret Cameron, Clementina Lady Hawarden, Lewis Carroll, and twentieth century photographers like Ann Brigman, Tina Modotti, Alfred Stieglitz, Margaret Watkins, Gertrude Kasebier, Lee Miller, and Sally Mann, plus family snapshot photo albums.

After a group lunch, participants will show their own work, guided by the instructor. Then the group will discuss levels of meaning in participants' work in relation to issues of private life which emerged in the morning session.
