Students will stay at the lovely, modernized 16th century Villa Maravegie, otherwise known as the Pensione Accademia.


After you register, you will receive information about all workshop details: maps, train schedules, a list of books about Venice, advice about what clothing, film, and photographic equipment to bring, and complete directions on how to get to the Pensione Accademia in Venice.


Holly Smith Pedlosky is an artist, teacher, and free lance photographer on Cape Cod where she lives. She speaks Italian fluently and knows Venice intimately. She has lived extensively in Venice and has been leading intensive photo workshops in Northern Italy since 1985.
Holly teaches photography at the Radcliffe Seminars at Harvard University and since 1993 she has taught workshops on Cape Cod, in Venice, and on Lake Como for the International Center of Photography. She has an international exhibition record, and has recently returned from a year in Venice, where she had a grant to photograph and videotape casalinghe, traditional Venetian housewives, and their art of hanging laundry.
