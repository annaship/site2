Fundamentals of Photography

@ Radcliffe Seminars
Radcliffe Institute for Advanced Study, Harvard University

Taught by Holly Smith Pedlosky

Seven Mondays, 10:30 am-1 pm. October 1-November 26, 2001
 

The goal of this short course is, to paraphrase the Joy of Cooking, to enable you to emerge as an strong, intuitive photographer on good speaking terms with your imagination. A second goal is to provide a supportive environment which will nourish photographic energies and ideas.

We will start with an inquiry into how photography emerged from Aristotle's observations of a solar eclipse, grew with Leonardo's desire to understand space, and came of age with the nineteenth century's passion for light.

You will learn how to work with the basic elements of photography: light, time, and sensitive emulsions. You will learn how to choose a camera and how to have a functional relationship with it: how your camera thinks, how to talk to it so it will listen to you, and how to listen when it is trying to talk to you. We will cover fundamental choices you must learn how to make: what films to use, how to get your work printed, how to use filters, f-stops, shutter speeds, tripods, and macro lenses. You will learn how to take powerful, well-exposed, well-composed photographs by design and not just by chance.

This Seminar will demonstrate and demystify the possibilities offered by both straight and digital photography.

No previous experience needed. Participants need to have a camera of any format, in excellent working condition.

Seven weeks, two and a half hours each session. Individual conferences with the instructor will be scheduled in addition.
