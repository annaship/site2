Venetian Light 2001 offers you a different Venice than you could find on your own or with any other group. You will discover an ancient civilization which is very vibrant in the present, whose children still speak the Venetian dialect, where 1,500 years of human stories speak to you from every wall and campo. This most public of cities also has its inner secrets, and with Holly Smith Pedlosky you will be able to enter gardens, palaces, and homes closed to the ordinary tourist.

At the end of 12 days of being an artist photographer in Venice you will feel, with Proust, that you have a new address.

 

Students will follow a Mediterranean rhythm: the class will meet in the mornings and late afternoons for technical sessions and for group critiques. Middays and evenings are free for eating, sleeping, exploring Venice, perhaps going to a Vivaldi concert in Vivaldi's church, and making new friends and new pictures. In addition, there will be ample free time to shoot and to have individual work review sessions with the instructors.

 

Lagoon Aphrodite

 

This is a workshop for all levels of photographers, beginner through advanced. Participants will be divided into two groups, depending on your skill level and what you want to learn.

Technical problems concerning basic camera skills (cameras, tripods, films, filters, depth of field, motion, macro photography, and the aperture-shutter speed trade-off) will be solved in slide talks and optional sessions.

 

The entire group will come together for field trips and critiques where the subjects of picture space, portraiture, landscape photography, aesthetic visualization, light and composition will be thoroughly discussed. The instructors will give daily assignments which will encourage each student to experience the city fully and to come up with an individual response.

A professional photo lab (color and black & white, all formats,) is available to process your work. You will need a 35mm camera, in good working condition. Other kinds of cameras, including point-and-shoots, large format cameras, and video camcorders, are also welcome.
