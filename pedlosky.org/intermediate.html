Intermediate Photography

Taught by Holly Smith Pedlosky
Radcliffe Seminars
Radcliffe Institute for Advanced Study, Harvard University

Spring, Mondays, 10:30-1 pm, February 4-April 1, 2002

This is a seminar for those who want to deepen their own connection to photography by an introduction to what has been done and what is being done by creative photographers.


For this workshop each student will bring in her or his own work in any photographic medium, (slides or prints, color or black and white, still or video,) in response to a weekly assignment. At the end of the course, each student will present a body of work in response to a chosen photographer. Each week there will be supportive critiques of student work in progress.


Simultaneously, this class will explore the history of nineteenth and twentieth century photography, through slide talks about selected photographers. Students will be encouraged to investigate parallel developments in the realms of history, psychology, philosophy, other visual arts, literature, drama, and opera.


The instructor will offer technical advice and information about camera work, composition, and digital imaging on the basis of students’ individual wants and needs.

A darkroom will also be available, upon the payment of a lab fee. This Seminar is open to intermediate to advanced photographers. Limited enrollment.
